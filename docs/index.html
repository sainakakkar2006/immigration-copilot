Why this exists

Most families do this alone.

Immigration attorneys cost more than many families can spend, so people navigate one of the most consequential processes of their lives with no help, in a language they didn't grow up speaking. The numbers say what that costs:

~30%
of immigrants with pending court cases have a lawyer. The rest face the system alone.
TRAC, Syracuse University
62% vs 27%
deportation rate without a lawyer, versus with one. Information changes outcomes.
American Immigration Council

We can't give anyone a lawyer. We can make sure nobody misses a deadline because they couldn't read the letter.

What it does

Five things, done carefully.

the flagship

Decode a letter

Snap a phone photo of any government notice. It reads the letter, tells you what it is, pulls out every deadline with days remaining, and gives you a calendar file with reminders.

every 2 days

News that actually matters

Real headlines from USCIS, the White House, and the Federal Register, explained in two plain sentences each. Filter by your visa type. No fear-mongering, no filler.

your case

Your situation, assessed

Describe where you stand, attach a document if you have one, and get an honest read: the issues, the urgency, the next steps, and the official links.

computed, not guessed

OPT deadline calculator

Every OPT and STEM OPT date, calculated in code from USCIS rules. The AI explains the dates; it never invents them. Download them straight to your calendar.

ready to file

Green card checklist

Forms, fees, every document for your specific case, the red flags to fix first, and a cover letter ready to edit. Download it and share it with your family.

always there

The essentials

The timeless rules for your visa category, like grace periods and filing windows, checked against USCIS.gov and shown even when there's no news at all.

In your language

The whole tool, not just a welcome page.

Every screen, every explanation, every checklist. Pick your language once and everything follows, with the terms from your official documents kept in English so they match what's in your hands.

Who made this

Two students. Same letters on our own kitchen tables.

We're Saina and Arsh. Our families went through this system, and we watched the people around us pay for answers that were sitting on USCIS.gov the whole time. Or worse, miss deadlines because a scary letter sat unopened in a drawer.

So we built the tool we wished our parents had: something that reads the letter, tells you the deadline, and says what to do next in the language you think in. It's free, it stays free, and every line of code is public. If it helps your family, that's the whole point.

The honest part

What you should know before trusting us.

  • This is not a law firm and it is not legal advice. Complicated case? Find a real lawyer, many are free.
  • Nothing you upload is stored. Documents are read in memory and discarded when you leave.
  • No account, no tracking, no immigration-status data kept. Ever.
  • Every fact links back to USCIS.gov, the State Department, or the Federal Register so you can check us.
  • Deadline math is computed in code from the regulations. The AI explains; it doesn't invent dates.
  • The code is open source. Found a mistake? Tell us and we'll fix it.
